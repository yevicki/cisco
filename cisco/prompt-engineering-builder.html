What makes a good system prompt?
Four ingredients: who the model is, what it can and can't do, how it should sound, and what to do when it's stuck. All four reduce variance across thousands of conversations.

What makes good few-shot examples?
Cover your most common case, an edge case, and a refusal (where the bot should say it doesn't know). Vary the phrasing. The model infers the pattern — not just the content.

When is chain-of-thought worth it?
Use it when correctness depends on multiple conditions — eligibility rules, multi-step policy lookups, compensation disputes. For simple factual Q&A, it adds unnecessary length.
